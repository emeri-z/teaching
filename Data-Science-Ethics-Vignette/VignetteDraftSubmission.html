Leaked alternative version to the campus wide email from UC Berkeley Chancellor  

March 13, 2020

Dear Campus Community,

In light of recent Covid-19 developments, we are writing with updates about the delivery of instruction for the duration of the semester, which will be continued via Zoom Video Communications.  

5 years ago, U.S. News & World Report asked ‘ "Do we need more technology in our schools?". To that, educators answered with a resounding: "yes." ‘. Technology enabled a more hands-on experience allowing for better understanding of concepts and theories, and 90% of U.S. educators noted modern technology is important to students’ success. Today, we are proud to announce that 100% of U.S. educators now note modern technology is essential to students’ success, as technology in the classroom paves a gateway to a brighter future. It is incredible to see our progress over the last two decades, as President Clinton imposed a “technology literacy” requirement on all middle school children in 1998, declaring “students should feel as comfortable with a keyboard as a chalkboard” - we have not only met that expectation, we have exceeded it, as 95% of our student body has yet to touch a chalkboard during their education at UC Berkeley, and now 100% of the student body are definitely comfortable with a keyboard, which is required for continuing your educations via Zoom teleconferencing technology.

Zoom is but the tip of the iceberg for a continued advanced technology revolution - Zoom offers end-to-end encrypted conference capabilities for over 700 universities, while Amazon ships toilet paper and essential goods to 50% of the entire US population, Instacart delivers billions of dollars worth of groceries in 3 weeks and single handedly boosting our GDP, Netflix keeps 169 million people from going insane from solitary confinement and boredom, and Coursera has 4-5x usual traffic as thousands of universities request free access to the platform thus free content for their curriculums and significantly reduced need for staff and educators, and Coursera assimilates universities by “helping them” transition entire classes online, thus on its way to becoming THE University. On the frontlines of data and healthcare materials in battling this pandemic, Automation Anywhere is becoming AI Anywhere and soon A.I., short for A(ll your data) I(n our systems), as frontrunners to aiding Asia in sharing data and statistics about coronavirus as early as December, while good samaritans like ON Semiconductors donate 10,000 masks to healthcare workers. Last but not least, Apple and Google provide life-saving insight into the spread of the virus.

Technology is becoming ubiquitous and powerful in providing US citizens resources, and we are so proud to be the #1 Public University in America, and tied for the #1 Computer Science program in the nation, training top talent graduates to join top-tier Big Tech companies such as the aforementioned. Our graduates are experts in algorithms motivated by their impact as cogs in a magnificent well-oiled machine of technology, becoming the largest contributors to societal good and overall well-being.

On the note of societal good, we would be remiss in not acknowledging another facet of technology - while healthcare workers fight the pandemic and treat patients, scientists at Gilead Pfizer and Moderna, among others, gruel away in labs to discover a vaccine that will end our strict social distancing measures and return our society to the norm. We are so proud to have Top 10 ranking programs in bioengineering, biology, and chemical engineering, yet again training top talent graduates to join top-tier Big Pharma companies, where they contribute to ground-breaking research and life-saving remedies released to the public with affordable price tags for all.

Our rigorous curricula across various disciplines all require ethics and social sciences courses to complete graduation requirements, and we continue this education on morals and ethics with a focus on technological issues such as privacy and security via the encrypted Zoom. We are proud that each and every student graduating from our fine institution is a well-rounded problem solving bound by their morals to follow their hearts in making the place a better place, shunning the idea of societal pressures of status, money, or “hype” as reasons for the career paths they choose.

We thank you all in maintaining instructional resilience, and for those of you graduating and seeking employment amidst this crisis, we urge you to remain resilient and contribute to our economy and your mental peace of mind by continuing to seek roles at Big Tech and Big Pharma companies which are increasing hiring during this time, bolstering our big corporations in hopes that we will repair the unemployment problem and help boost our economy by supporting the stock market as media releases of technological developments assuage the fears of consumers and business-owners alike, and consumers race to invest their assets into the most profitable and powerful companies, making them even more profitable and powerful in order to uphold the entirety of our health well-being in fighting this pandemic, and stepping in when our federal government does not have the technological expertise nor resources nor foresight to step in to provide successful infrastructure supporting healthcare and employment.

We will continue to update you on this issue with information posted on the new coronavirus information website. Information on the virus and related health and safety information is available on the special University Health Services website. Students, please share this message with your family and any concerned parties.

Sincerely,

Eric Yuan, Zoom CEO
